Why It Matters

The 5 Moments for Hand Hygiene

Based on the WHO standard — know exactly when to clean your hands in clinical practice.

1

Before Patient Contact

Prevent transferring germs from the environment to the patient.

2

Before Aseptic Task

Protect the patient from organisms during invasive procedures.

3

After Body Fluid Exposure

Protect yourself and the environment from patient germs.

4

After Patient Contact

Protect yourself after touching the patient.

5

After Patient Surroundings

Protect yourself after touching objects in the patient zone.

Learning Modules

Hand Hygiene Techniques

Watch the demonstration videos then follow the step-by-step guides. Complete each module to unlock its quiz set.

💧

Step 1 – Wet Hands

Wet hands with clean running water (warm or cold) and apply enough soap.

🤲

Step 2 – Lather Palms

Rub palms together vigorously to build a full lather on all surfaces.

🔄

Step 3 – Interlace Fingers

Interlace fingers, rub between them and on the back of each hand.

✋

Step 4 – Back of Hands

Rub the back of each hand using the opposite palm with interlaced fingers.

🤞

Step 5 – Thumbs

Clasp each thumb with the opposite hand and rub rotationally.

🖐

Step 6 – Fingertips

Rub fingertips in a circular motion against the opposite palm.

🚿

Step 7 – Rinse Thoroughly

Rinse all surfaces under clean running water. Total: 40–60 seconds

🗒

Step 8 – Dry & Turn Off Tap

Dry with a single-use towel, then use it to turn off the tap.

💊

Step 1 – Apply Product

Apply a palmful of alcohol-based hand rub to cover all surfaces.

🤲

Step 2 – Palm to Palm

Rub palms together in a circular motion until evenly spread.

🔄

Step 3 – Between Fingers

Interlace fingers and rub between them thoroughly.

✋

Step 4 – Back of Hands

Rub backs of hands with opposing palms, fingers interlaced.

🤞

Step 5 – Thumbs

Clasp each thumb and rub rotationally until covered.

⏳

Step 6 – Air Dry

Rub until hands are completely dry. Do NOT wipe off. Total: 20–30 seconds

🏥

Before Patient Contact

Decontaminate before entering any patient care area or touching the patient.

💉

Before Invasive Procedures

Clean hands before inserting catheters, IV lines, or performing wound care.

🩺

After Physical Exam

Always decontaminate after touching or examining a patient.

🧤

After Removing Gloves

Gloves have micro-perforations. Hand hygiene is still required after removal.

🗑

After Handling Waste

Body fluids, soiled linen, and clinical waste require immediate decontamination.

🍽

Before & After Eating

Prevents cross-contamination between the clinical environment and food.

🛏

After Patient Surroundings

Clean hands after touching bedside tables, rails, or any item in the patient zone.

🔬

After Handling Specimens

Laboratory specimens require hand hygiene even if gloves were worn.

Impact

What Happens When We Don't Comply?

🦠

Healthcare-Associated Infections

HAIs affect 1 in 10 hospitalized patients globally — most are preventable.

📈

Antibiotic Resistance

Spreading resistant organisms creates infections that are harder and costlier to treat.

💔

Patient Harm & Mortality

Poor hand hygiene remains a leading cause of preventable patient deaths worldwide.

💸

Economic Burden

HAIs cost healthcare systems billions annually in extended stays and treatments.
